Laser system for biomimetic surfaces

Optical unit

A laser system comprised (i) a femtosecond-pulsed laser oscillator, (ii) an x–y galvano mirror, (iii) a telecentric fq lens, an x–y electric-stage, and (v) a z-stage.

Typically, these control devices are controlled by sequencers, but we perform feedback control using programming languages with our own control algorithms.

For shaft processing

A chuck-equipped rotation mechanism for processing the axis can also be mounted on the x-y-z stage.

Processing Results of PTFE

When a hydrophobic texture is processed into PTFE, water droplets roll off. Such a surface is expected to have effects such as self-cleaning properties, stain resistance, anti-fogging properties, and reduced resistance to liquid transport.

On surfaces processed with hydrophobic texture, it can be observed that droplets tend to jump more easily compared to untreated surfaces.

Droplet bouncing phenomena can be observed with various types of liquids, not just water. These include human plasma, for instance. In other words, these technologies can also be applied in the medical field.
